1.2 Methods to study behavior observation, interview, correlation, and experimental
method

Observation method :

#1 Naturalistic observation :

observing the subject at their natural state, like animals in the jungle . the observer has no control over the situation. the primary goal is to observe genuine behaviors in a real world setting

#2 Controlled observation :

when the observer is completely in control of the situation , like a lab

#3 Non participant observation :

observing from far away that the subject doesn’t know that he’s under observation. but in naturalistic the observer may or may not interact but no manipulation and control. but non participant can happen in anywhere. (Covert : being a ninja , unknown | opposite word : overt)

#4 Participant observation :

observer becomes one of the group

Steps :

  • Decide what to watch: Choose what you want to observe.
  • Choose where to watch: Pick a place to observe.
  • Create a plan: Decide how you'll record what you see.
  • Practice: Try out your plan before starting.
  • Start watching: Observe and take notes.
  • Analyze what you saw: Look at your notes and figure out what they mean.
  • Share your findings: Tell others about what you learned.

Merits and Demerits of Observational Method in Psychology

MeritsDemerits
Naturalistic Setting Behavior is observed in its natural context, providing a more realistic and authentic view.Observer Bias The observer's own beliefs, expectations, or values can influence their observations and interpretations.
Direct Observation Behavior is directly witnessed, minimizing reliance on self-report or other indirect measures.Lack of Control It's difficult to control extraneous variables that might affect behavior, making it challenging to establish causality.
Descriptive Data Observational methods can provide valuable descriptions of behavior and phenomena.Subjectivity Observations can be subjective, and different observers might interpret behavior differently.
Hypothesis Generation Observations can lead to new research questions and hypotheses.Time-Consuming Observational studies can be time-consuming, especially for long-term or infrequent behaviors.
Ethical Advantages In some cases, observational methods may be less invasive than experiments or surveys.Limited Generalizability Findings from observational studies might not be easily generalizable to larger populations.
Experimental method
  • most scientific and objective method
  • used to study cause and effect relationship regarding the nature of human behavior

3 important variables:

  1. independent variable (DV):
    This is the variable that is measured and observed. It is expected to change in response to the manipulation of the independent variable.
    Variable controlled and manipulated by the observer
    {eg : effect of smoking on cancer. smoking is independent variable}
  1. dependent variable (DV): This is the variable that is measured and observed. It is expected to change in response to the manipulation of the independent variable.
    variable that changes depending on the independent variable
    {cancer}
  1. Control Variable: These are variables that are held constant throughout the experiment to ensure that they do not influence the relationship between the IV and DV. By controlling these variables, the experimenter can establish a stronger causal relationship between the IV and DV.

Correlation method

A correlation method is a statistical technique used to measure the relationship between two or more variables. In psychological research, it's often used to investigate the strength and direction of associations between psychological constructs.
